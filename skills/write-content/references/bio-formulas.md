# Bio Writing Formulas

8 proven formulas for social media bios across platforms.

## Character limits by platform

| Platform | Bio limit | Post/Additional limits |
|----------|-----------|------------------------|
| Twitter/X | 160 chars | Display name: 50. Handle: 15. Post: 280 (free) / 25,000 (Premium) |
| GitHub | 160 chars | README.md: no practical limit |
| LinkedIn headline | 220 chars | About/Summary: 2,600 chars |
| Instagram | 150 chars | Caption: 2,200 chars (125 shown before fold) |
| TikTok | 80 chars | Username: 24. Captions: 4,000 |
| Discord About Me | 190 chars | Messages: 2,000 (free) / 4,000 (Nitro). Status: 100 |
| Facebook Intro | 101 chars | Posts: 63,206 chars |
| Bluesky | 256 chars | Posts: 300 chars |
| Threads | Same as Instagram (150) | Posts: 500 chars |
| YouTube About | 1,000 chars | Video titles: 100 (70 shown). Descriptions: 5,000 (157 shown) |
| Pinterest | 160 chars | Board names: 50. Pin descriptions: 500 |

Emojis count as 2 characters each. Always count before delivering.

## The 8 formulas

### 1. Role + Specialty + Personality
`[Role] | [Specialty] | [Personality trait]`

"Full Stack Developer | React & Node.js | Open source enthusiast 🚀"
"iOS Engineer | SwiftUI nerd | Building apps nobody asked for"
"Data Engineer | dbt + Snowflake | Automate everything"

### 2. Building + Previously + Achievement
`Building [X] | Previously [Y] | [Achievement]`

"Building @ProductName | Previously @BigCo | Y Combinator W24"
"Building tools for devs | Ex-Stripe | 50K+ GitHub stars"
"Building [AppName] | Ex-Google | 10M+ users served"

### 3. What + For Whom + Proof
`[What you do] for [who] | [Social proof]`

"Helping B2B founders write content that converts | $4M+ in client revenue"
"Teaching developers to ship faster | 10K+ students"
"Writing tutorials for self-taught devs | 500K+ monthly readers"

### 4. Mission/Value
`[Aspirational mission] | [What you share] | [CTA]`

"Making AI accessible for everyone | Co-founder @company"
"Writing code that doesn't suck | Sharing what I learn along the way"
"On a mission to democratize AI education | Co-founder @company"

### 5. Audience-First/Question
`[Question targeting pain point] | [Credential]`

"Struggling to grow on LinkedIn? I've helped 500+ creators 10x their reach"
"Want to learn Rust? I write the tutorials I wish existed"
"Burnt out on funding pitches? I help founders raise without the BS"

### 6. Multi-Hat/Slash
`[Role 1] / [Role 2] / [Role 3] | [Unifying theme]`

"Engineer. Writer. Educator. | Making complex things simple."
"Founder / Developer / Designer | Building @AppName"
"Developer / Educator / Runner | Building in public"

### 7. Story Arc
`[Origin] → [Current state] | [Differentiator]`

"Teacher to Tech 👨🏻‍💻 in 1.5 yrs | Now building @AppName"
"Self-taught → Senior Engineer | Sharing the path"
"Dropout → CTO | The journey beats the destination"

### 8. Achievement + CTA
`[Impressive credential/number] | [What you share]`

"10+ years in design. 20M+ views online. Built 3 profitable products."
"Shipped 47 open source packages. Writing about what I learned."

## Bio writing rules

1. Show personality, not just credentials. "Open source enthusiast" says more about you than another job title.
2. Use the pipe character (|) or periods to separate sections. Not commas or slashes between every element.
3. One CTA max. Don't put both a link and "DM me" and "Subscribe."
4. At most 1–2 emojis. They count as 2 chars each.
5. Lead with your strongest identifier. What would make someone follow you?
6. Platform-match the tone: Twitter = personality, LinkedIn = professional, GitHub = developer.
7. Avoid: "Passionate about…" "Aspiring…" "Lover of…" These are filler.
8. Include what you share/post about, not just who you are. People follow for future value.
